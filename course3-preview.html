Step 1: Choose Your AI Base (5 minutes)

Option A: ChatGPT (Recommended for most people)

  • Free tier works fine to start
  • Plus ($20/mo) is worth it if you use it daily
  • Best for: General business tasks, email, content

Option B: Claude

  • Free tier is generous
  • Pro ($20/mo) for heavy use
  • Best for: Long documents, detailed writing, analysis

Option C: Google Gemini

  • Free with Google account
  • Best for: People already deep in Google Workspace

Pick one. Don't overthink it. You can always switch later. For this guide, I'll use ChatGPT as the example, but the concepts work with any of them.

Step 2: Train Your Assistant on Your Business (15 minutes)

This is the step most people skip — and it's why their AI gives generic garbage answers.

Create Your Business Brief

Open ChatGPT. Start a new conversation. Paste this (fill in the brackets):

I want you to act as my business assistant. Here's everything you need to know about my business:

BUSINESS NAME: [Your business name]
WHAT WE DO: [One paragraph description]
OUR CUSTOMERS: [Who buys from you]
OUR MAIN SERVICES/PRODUCTS:
1. [Service 1] - [Price]
2. [Service 2] - [Price]  
3. [Service 3] - [Price]

OUR HOURS: [Business hours]
OUR LOCATION: [If applicable]
CONTACT INFO: [Phone, email, website]

COMMON QUESTIONS WE GET:
1. [Question] → [How you usually answer]
2. [Question] → [How you usually answer]
3. [Question] → [How you usually answer]
4. [Question] → [How you usually answer]
5. [Question] → [How you usually answer]

OUR TONE: [Professional/casual/friendly/etc]
THINGS TO NEVER SAY: [Any topics or claims to avoid]

Remember this information for our entire conversation. When I ask you to draft responses, use this context.

Save This as a Custom GPT (Optional but Powerful)

If you have ChatGPT Plus:

  1. Go to chatgpt.com/create
  2. Paste your business brief as the instructions
  3. Name it "[Your Business] Assistant"
  4. Now it ALWAYS knows your business — no re-explaining

Step 3: Build Your Email Template Library (15 minutes)

Ask your AI assistant to generate responses for your most common scenarios:

Based on my business info, draft email responses for these scenarios. Make each one professional but friendly, under 100 words:

1. New inquiry asking about pricing
2. Scheduling a consultation/meeting
3. Follow-up after no response (gentle)
4. Follow-up after no response (firmer, 2nd attempt)
5. Thank you after completing a job
6. Responding to a complaint
7. Asking for a review/testimonial
8. Answering "do you offer [service you don't offer]?"
9. Holiday/out-of-office auto-reply
10. Responding to a referral

Save these templates. Create a Google Doc or folder called "Email Templates." You'll use these daily.

Pro Move: Create Quick-Response Shortcuts

For each template, ask the AI to create a SHORT version (2-3 sentences) for text/DM responses, and a FULL version for email.

Step 4: Set Up Automated Email Drafts (15 minutes)

Using Gmail + ChatGPT

  1. When a new email comes in, copy the relevant part
  2. Paste into ChatGPT: "Draft a reply to this email: [paste]"
  3. AI generates response using your business context
  4. Copy, paste into Gmail, review, send

That's the manual version. It still saves time because the AI writes 90% of the response.

Level Up: Make.com Automation (Free)

  1. Sign up at make.com (free tier = 1,000 ops/month)
  2. Create scenario: Gmail → OpenAI → Gmail Draft
  3. AI automatically drafts replies to incoming emails
  4. You just review and hit send

This turns a 5-minute email into a 30-second review. Across 20+ emails a day, that's hours saved.

Step 5: Automate Scheduling (10 minutes)

Option A: Calendly (Free tier)

  1. Sign up at calendly.com
  2. Set your availability
  3. Share your link: calendly.com/yourname
  4. When someone asks to meet, send the link instead of playing email tag

Option B: AI + Calendar

Ask your AI assistant:

Someone wants to schedule a call. My availability this week is:
- Tuesday 2-5 PM
- Wednesday 10 AM - 12 PM
- Thursday 1-4 PM

Draft a response offering these times and include a professional sign-off.

The Power Combo

Add your Calendly link to your AI email templates. Now when someone inquires about a service, the AI drafts a response that includes your booking link. Zero back-and-forth.

Step 6: Never Lose a Lead (10 minutes)

The Follow-Up System

Create a simple spreadsheet (Google Sheets): | Name | Email | Date Contacted | Service Interested | Status | Next Follow-Up |

Set AI Reminders

Every Monday morning, paste your spreadsheet data into ChatGPT:

Here are my open leads. Draft follow-up emails for anyone I haven't contacted in 5+ days. Make each one personal based on their service interest.

Level Up: Automate It

In Make.com, create a scenario that:

  1. Checks your leads spreadsheet weekly
  2. Identifies anyone without recent contact
  3. Drafts a follow-up email
  4. Saves as Gmail draft for your review

Step 7: Your Daily AI Routine

Morning (5 minutes)

  1. Check email drafts your AI prepared overnight
  2. Review and send (or tweak)
  3. Check lead follow-up list

During the Day (as needed)

  1. New inquiry? → AI drafts the response
  2. Need to write something? → AI gets you 90% there
  3. Customer question? → Check your template library first

Weekly (15 minutes)

  1. Update your leads spreadsheet
  2. Ask AI to analyze: "Which leads are going cold? Draft re-engagement emails."
  3. Review and improve your templates based on what's working

Cheat Sheet: AI Prompts for Every Situation

Situation Prompt
New inquiry "Draft a response to this inquiry about [service]. Include pricing and next steps."
Follow-up "Draft a follow-up email. Tone: friendly, not pushy. Mention [previous conversation detail]."
Proposal "Write a project proposal for [scope]. Include timeline, deliverables, pricing at $[X]."
Complaint "Draft a professional response to this complaint. Acknowledge the issue, apologize, offer [solution]."
Review request "Draft a message asking [client name] for a Google/Yelp review after completing [project]."
Social post "Write a [platform] post about [topic]. Keep it under [X] words. Include a call to action."
Invoice follow-up "Draft a polite payment reminder for invoice #[X], [amount], due [date]."
